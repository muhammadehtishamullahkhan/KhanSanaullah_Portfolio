Brief Introduction

An urban mobility researcher pursuing a Master's degree in Urban Science and Policy at Tokyo Metropolitan University. My work combines spatiotemporal analytics, GIS, and behavioural modelling to study micromobility, bicycle-sharing systems, and urban travel behaviour. With urban planning experience in Pakistan, I bridge practice and research to improve safety, equity, and reliability in cities.

Research
Published Research Papers

  • Khan, M. S. U., et al. (2025)

    Understanding school zone driving behavior using structural equation modeling based on the extended theory of planned behavior.

    This study investigates the psychological factors influencing driver behavior in school zones using an extended Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB). By applying structural equation modeling (SEM), the research identifies key predictors of compliance with speed limits and safety protocols, providing insights for urban policy and road safety management.

    Discover Cities, 2(1), 112 First Author Corresponding Author View Paper

  • Aziz, A., et al. (2025)

    Assessment of constraints in use of non-motorized transport in Lahore.

    Lahore faces significant challenges in promoting non-motorized transport (NMT). This research assesses the physical, social, and institutional constraints preventing the adoption of cycling and walking, suggesting a framework for more inclusive and sustainable urban mobility infrastructure.

    Case Studies on Transport Policy, 101503 Corresponding Author View Research

  • Hameed, R., et al. (2025)

    How Pannah Gahs (shelter homes) are helping homeless people in Lahore, Pakistan.

    An evaluative study on the performance and impact of state-run shelter homes (Pannah Gahs). The research uses qualitative and quantitative methods to determine how these facilities address homelessness and what improvements are needed in service delivery and urban welfare policy.

    Journal of Housing and the Built Environment Corresponding Author View Research

  • Saqib, A., et al. (2024)

    Bridging nature and urbanity through green roof resilience framework (GRF): A thematic review.

    A comprehensive thematic review of green roof implementations and their role in urban resilience. The study proposes a Green Roof Resilience Framework (GRF) to integrate nature-based solutions into modern urban planning to mitigate heat islands and manage stormwater.

    Nature-Based Solutions, 100182 Co-author View Research

  • Aziz, A., & Khan, M. S. U. (2023)

    Examining the relationship between compact urban form and quality of life in Lahore.

    This research explores how urban density and compact city models influence the perceived quality of life in rapidly growing South Asian metropolises. It provides a comparative analysis of different residential patterns and their accessibility to essential urban services.

    UEF5 + World Planning Congress 59, Canada Co-author View Research
